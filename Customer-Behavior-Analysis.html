Customer Behavior Analysis Explained

Shopping Behavior has become a subject of of supreme importance for businesses competiveness. Every click, every purchase and decision made by customer leaves a trail of data. This data when used effectively can unlock consumer insights and market trends. There are so many companies that struggle to maintain their customers. Poor product or service quality, negative customer experience and lack of engagement are some factors that lead customers to leave a company and search for a better experience in another. This not only causes a lose in the revenue of the organisation but it also benefits the competitors of your business. 

Except from the obvious reasons (revenue increase) it is important to keep your existing customers satisfied due to:
Cost-effectiveness: New customers can be expensive on account of marketing costs and sales efforts. Keeping your old customers, generally, costs less, as you already spend money to find them in first place. 
Word-of-mouth marketing: Satisfied customers are the best marketing tool you can have. Positive comments can bring new customers with less cost than tranditional marketing. 
Feedback: Existing customers can provide valuable tips and improvements to upgrade your product/service. ****

Why is Customer Behavior Analysis important for Businesses?

The top reasons why a customer behavior analysis is a must-have for businesses are:

  • Identifing patterns helps you make accurate predictions for the future.
  • Eximining in depth current customer behavior helps you win over new customers.
  • Personilize customer experiences drive sales.
  • Understanding customer behavior and which factors influence them, helps you to increase customer retention.
    Therefore, it arrises the question, how raw data can transform into intriguing insights which will help businessed to increase their revenue?

Which are the Objectives that will help us to create a Customer Behavior Analysis?

The first thing that we have to do is to establish the initial objectives. Before we start gathering our data it is important to know what we want to know about customer’s behavior. Which are the keys that will help us to unlock important insights and what informations I need to know in order to have answers about our customer’s behavior.

How to Measure Customers’ Behavior?

Qualitative Attributes

First, we need to know how our ideal customer look like, who they are, where do they live, how old they are. Also, it is important to know their income, education, social status, gender, demography. To acquire a complete picture of these personalities, we must combine customer data from internal and external sources.

Quantitative Attributes

Quantitative are the data that can be easily quantified, compared and analysed. They usually include informations such as purchased history, website visits and views, social media management, conversions reports for marketing/sales activity.

Types of Customer Behavior Analysis

RFM Analysis (Recency/Frequency/Monetary Value)

RFM Analysis, is a type of customer segmentation and behavioral marketing used to help businesses rank and segment customers based on the recency, frequency and monetary value of a transaction.

Customer Lifetime Value (CLV Models)

Measures: A RFM analysis measures which customers are of highest and lowest value to an organization based on purchase recency, frequency and monetary value.
Gives insights into: How to personalize offers? By creating effective customer segments, you can create relevant personalized offers. How to improve conversion Rates? Personalized offers will yield higher conversion rates because your customers are enganging with products they care about.

Customer Lifetime Value is the total revenue or profit generated by a customer over the entire course of their relationship with the business The higher the CLV, the more valuable a buyer is to the company, as they would generate more revenue and are more likely to be loyal.

Measures: Gives insights into how much of your revenue comes from repeat customers vs. new customers. This is an important figure to know because it helps you make decisions about how much money to invest in acquiring new customers and retaining existing ones. 
Gives insights into: How much you can afford to spend on sales and marketing? What revenue can you predict in the future? How can you optimize acquisition cost for the maximum value? How much money should you invest in retention?

Retention Analysis

The key to getting the most of your retention analysis is conducting it regularly. Customer behavior changes over time and so does your product offering. Seeing the full picture is only possible when you analyze customer cohorts consistently and continuously. To calculate retention rate, divide your active users that continue their subscriptions at the end of a given period by the total number of active users you had at the beginning of that time period.

Measures: How long do the customers stay with your business as well as how much of your revenue comes from repeat customers vs new customers. 
Gives insights into: Those insights helps to generate more revenue and profit, apply marketing strategies more effectively.

Churn Analysis

Churn analysis is the process of using churn data to understand, how many customers leave over a period of time or how much revenue a company loses over a period of time.

Measures: How long does it take until a customer stops using your product? Are there certain segments which show a higher propensity than others? What is the impact of seasonal effects? 
Gives insights into: When the drop of happens? Who are those customers who are less likely to stay with your business?
